1Define a polynomial.
A polynomial is an expression consisting of variables and coefficients combined using addition, subtraction, and multiplication with non-negative integer exponents.
2Difference between linear and quadratic equations?
Linear equations have degree 1; quadratic equations have degree 2. Linear graphs are straight lines, quadratic graphs are parabolas.
3Define factorization.
Factorization is expressing an expression as a product of its factors that multiply to give the original expression.
4What is the quadratic formula?
For ax² + bx + c = 0, the solutions are x = [-b ± √(b²-4ac)] / (2a).
5What is a derivative?
It measures the rate at which a function changes at a given point; slope of the tangent line.
6What is integration?
Integration is the reverse of differentiation; calculates area under a curve or accumulation of quantities.
7State the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.
It connects differentiation and integration: the derivative of an integral of a function gives back the function.
8Define matrix multiplication.
A product of two matrices where each element is the sum of products of corresponding row and column elements.
9What is the determinant of a matrix?
A scalar value that can be computed from a square matrix and indicates if the matrix is invertible (non-zero) or singular (zero).
10What is an eigenvalue?
A scalar λ for which there exists a non-zero vector v such that Av = λv for a square matrix A.
11What is probability?
It quantifies the likelihood of an event happening, ranging from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).
12Define random variable.
A variable that takes on numerical values determined by the outcome of a random experiment.
13Difference between discrete and continuous distribution?
Discrete variables take countable values; continuous variables take values in a continuous range.
14Define prime number.
A natural number greater than 1 that has no positive divisors other than 1 and itself.
15What is a composite number?
A natural number greater than 1 that is not prime; it has divisors other than 1 and itself.
16Define Pythagoras theorem.
In a right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of squares of the other two sides: c² = a² + b².
17What are sine, cosine, tangent?
Trigonometric ratios of angles in a right triangle: sin = opposite/hypotenuse, cos = adjacent/hypotenuse, tan = opposite/adjacent.
18Define combinatorics.
The study of counting, arrangement, and combination of objects.
19What is a graph in discrete math?
A set of vertices connected by edges, used to model relationships and networks.
20Define mathematical induction.
A method of proving statements for all natural numbers by proving base case and inductive step.
